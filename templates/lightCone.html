Light Cone 3D Visualization

This interactive 3D graph represents a set of parametric curves generated by the equations:

$$ x(t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - t^2}} (a - tb), \quad y(t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - t^2}} (at + b), \quad z(t) = t $$

These equations are a part of Lorentz transformation, which describes how space and time coordinates change between observers moving relative to each other at a constant velocity. In special relativity, the transformation is given by:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} x' \\ t' \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \gamma & -\gamma v/c \\ -\gamma v/c & \gamma \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ t \end{bmatrix} $$

where $$ \gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}} $$ is the Lorentz factor, $$ v $$ is the velocity of the moving observer, and $$ c $$ is the speed of light.

The plotted parametric curves demonstrate how time and space coordinates transform due to the speed of the observer. As velocity increases, the perception of simultaneity and distance shifts, affecting the structure of spacetime. The light cone structure represents the limit where information can travel, ensuring causality in physics.
